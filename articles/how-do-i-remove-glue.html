How do you remove dried glue?

Removing dried glue can be a tricky task, but with the right approach, you can get the job done quickly and easily. First, you will need to soften the glue. This can be done by using a hair dryer or by soaking the area in warm, soapy water. Once the glue has been softened, you can start to scrape it away with a putty knife or similar tool. Be careful not to scratch the surface of whatever you are trying to remove the glue from. If you have stubborn dried glue still remaining, you can try using a stronger solvent such as acetone. Be sure to test it in a small, inconspicuous area first to make sure it won't damage the surface. Apply the solvent with a cloth or brush and let it sit for a few minutes before wiping it away. Once you have removed the glue, you can clean the area with warm, soapy water and a soft cloth. This will help ensure that all of the glue has been removed and the area is clean. If you are still having trouble getting all of the glue off, you may want to consider using a commercial adhesive remover. These products are designed to safely and effectively remove dried glue and other adhesives. With these steps, you should be able to easily remove dried glue from any surface. Just be sure to take your time and take the necessary safety precautions.
